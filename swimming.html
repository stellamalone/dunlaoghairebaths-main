Tradition of bathing

Bathing has been a favourite activity since the 1700s. Back then, it was more than just swimming. People loved it for staying healthy, relaxing, and hanging out with friends. It was a special way to enjoy nature and feel good.

At Dun Laoghaire Baths, we keep this old tradition alive. Here, you can feel the history in the waves and hear it in the happy sounds of people swimming. Bathing used to be something only fancy people did, but now everyone enjoys it. It's all about having a good time, feeling well, and enjoying life's simple moments.

When you swim with us, you're joining a long story that started a long time ago. Dun Laoghaire Baths is proud of this story, and we're excited for you to be a part of it. Join in, feel the history, and have fun in this age-old tradition of bathing.

Discover the benefits of bathing

Bathing isn't just about getting wet, it's great for you in many ways

Keeps you healthy

Bathing is fantastic for your physical health. It gets your body moving and your heart pumping, improving circulation and cardiovascular health. The natural resistance of water makes it a gentle yet effective way to exercise, perfect for all ages and fitness levels.

Stress relief

Immerse yourself into the water and let your stress melt away. The calming sound of the waves and the soothing rhythm of swimming can help clear your mind, reduce anxiety, and promote a sense of inner peace.

Social interaction

Bathing is a wonderful way to connect with friends and family or meet new people. It's a social activity that brings people together, creating memories and strengthening community bonds.

Connection with nature

Being in and around water allows you to connect with nature, enjoy the fresh air, and soak in the beautiful surroundings. It's a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

Start your swimming journey now

Are you ready to dive into swimming but not sure how to begin?
Here's how you can dive in and make the most of your swimming experience.

Seasonal swim

Each season brings unique swim experiences: summer's warm waters for beginners, spring/fall's cool swims, and winter's crisp waters for adventure and health.

Duration in water

For new swimmers or cold seasons, begin with brief sessions to acclimate. In warm water, extend swim time as comfortable, but avoid overexertion.

Start slow

Start in shallow water, gradually acclimating yourself. Practice floating, gentle kicking, and simple strokes to increase water confidence, comfort, and swimming proficiency.

Dry off quickly

As soon as you exit the water, dry yourself thoroughly. It's essential to get out of your wet swimwear as soon as possible to stop the evaporation process, which can make you feel colder.

Dress in layers

Have warm clothing ready for post-swim. Layering is crucial. It facilitates easy adjustment to body temperature changes, ensuring comfort and warmth.

Sip warm fluid

Drink a warm beverage like herbal tea, warm water, or a sports drink. It not only helps to warm you from the inside but also assists in rehydration. 

Move around

Do gentle exercise to generate body heat. Walking, stretching, or gentle jogging on the spot can help increase circulation and warm your body.

Stay safe

Follow swimming rules, listen to lifeguards, and never swim alone. Respect your limits and prioritize safety, especially in cold months.
