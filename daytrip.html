Dún Laoghaire Landmarks and Museums

Dún Laoghaire Pier

Dún Laoghaire Pier is a Victorian pier that extends into Dublin Bay. The East Pier is a popular walking spot offering scenic views of Dublin Bay, leading to the lighthouse at its end. It's a focal point for leisure activities and offers a panoramic view of the sea and the town.

Maritime Museum

Housed in the 180-year-old Mariners’ Church, the museum displays maritime artefacts including the Baily Optic Light and exhibits related to the Titanic. Guided tours are available.

The People’s Park

Developed in the late 19th century, this Victorian-style park features a gardener’s residence turned office, a Victorian shelter now serving as a tearoom, two cast iron fountains, a bandstand with original gaslight standards, a garden for the blind, a children's playground, and public toilets.

Royal Marine Hotel

Overlooking Dublin Bay, this historic hotel has hosted notable figures like Frank Sinatra and Charlie Chaplin. Originally opened in 1865, it now operates as a luxury 4-star hotel.

The Martello Tower

This 19th-century tower, set in Sandycove, is famous for its association with James Joyce's "Ulysses". It now serves as a museum dedicated to Joyce's life and work, offering free admission.

The Library

The library in Dún Laoghaire, known as the dlr LexIcon, is the main public library and cultural center in the Dún Laoghaire-Rathdown area. Opened in December 2014, the LexIcon represents a significant investment in public infrastructure and cultural development.

The People’s Park Sunday Market

A vibrant Sunday market located on the edge of Dún Laoghaire town centre, within the historic People’s Park. This market operates every Sunday from 10am to 5pm and features over 50 vendors each week.

The market offers a wide range of products, including hot food, baked goods, art and crafts, artisan food products, and seasonal produce. It's a fantastic place for tasting local food, buying hand-made products like jewellery, soap, honey, and even books in a relaxed and entertaining atmosphere.

The market has been relaunched to include vendors trading from around the Lexicon to the Metals down to the People’s Park, encompassing artisan and crafts along with a Food Village. The summer trading hours are from 10.00am to 4pm, providing something for everyone.

Getting here

Get to Dun Laoghaire Baths by bus, train, or car. We provide detailed directions and parking information.

By Train: The DART (Dublin Area Rapid Transit) system is the quickest way, taking about 16 minutes for the journey. The DART runs from various stations in Dublin City Centre to Dun Laoghaire station. These include Pearse, Connolly and Tara Street stations. This option offers stunning views of Dublin Bay along the way. Trains run every 10 minutes during rush hour and every 20 minutes during off-peak times

By Bus: Dublin Bus provides services that connect Dublin City Centre with Dun Laoghaire. The bus journey can take approximately 27 minutes, with buses running frequently from different locations within the city. Nightlink services are also available for late-night travel

By Taxi or Car: For a more direct route, taking a taxi or driving from Dublin City Centre to Dun Laoghaire is an option. The journey by car can take around 13 minutes, depending on traffic conditions
